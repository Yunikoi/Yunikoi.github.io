第一组：基础词性与时态 (1-10)

  1. The marketing director _ the final draft of the proposal yesterday.
    (A) approve (B) approved (C) approves (D) approving

  2. Please provide a _ estimate of the project costs by Friday afternoon.
    (A) precision (B) precisely (C) precise (D) preciseness

  3. Mr. Kim is _ for overseeing the merger between the two firms.
    (A) responsibility (B) responsible (C) responsibly (D) responsibilities

  4. The new software allows employees to complete tasks _ than before.
    (A) quick (B) quickest (C) more quickly (D) as quick as

  5. Most of the delegates _ at the hotel since last Monday.
    (A) stay (B) are staying (C) have been staying (D) will stay

  6. The HR department requested that all staff _ their contact information.
    (A) update (B) updates (C) updated (D) updating

  7. We are looking for a candidate who can work _ under pressure.
    (A) effect (B) effective (C) effectively (D) effectiveness

  8. The supervisor _ the team for their hard work during the gala.
    (A) commended (B) commend (C) commending (D) was commended

  9. Visitors are reminded to park _ in the designated guest area.
    (A) exclusive (B) exclusively (C) exclusion (ed) excluded

  10. The production line _ if the maintenance team doesn’t arrive soon.
    (A) stops (B) will stop (C) stopped (D) has stopped

第二组：介词、连词与代词 (11-20)

  1. The workshop was canceled _ the low number of registrations.
    (A) because (B) although (C) due to (D) despite

  2. Ms. Lee prepared the presentation _ without any assistance.
    (A) she (B) her (C) hers (D) herself

  3. The merger will be finalized _ the board members reach an agreement.
    (A) once (B) during (C) instead (D) whether

  4. Please keep your receipts _ you can be reimbursed for travel expenses.
    (A) so that (B) in order to (C) because of (D) as if

  5. Neither the manager _ the employees were aware of the power outage.
    (A) or (B) nor (C) and (D) but

  6. The office will be closed for renovations _ July 1st to July 10th.
    (A) at (B) from (C) on (D) in

  7. Mr. Tanaka is the person _ is in charge of the international accounts.
    (A) whom (B) whose (C) who (D) which

  8. _ the bad weather, the outdoor company picnic was a great success.
    (A) Although (B) Despite (C) Even though (D) However

  9. Every employee must sign _ contract before starting work.
    (A) they (B) their (C) them (D) theirs

  10. The application process is _ simple that it takes only five minutes.
    (A) such (B) very (C) so (D) too

Passage 1: Strategic Merger and Operational Transition (Double Passage)

Difficulty: High (Focus on inference and specific financial terminology)

Document 1: Internal Memorandum

To: All Senior Staff, Arisaka Tech Solutions
From: Kenji Sato, CEO
Date: October 12, 2026
Subject: Finalization of Acquisition

I am pleased to announce that our acquisition of Lumina Software is now legally complete. While this merger positions us as a leader in AI-driven logistics, the transition period will require rigorous discipline. Effective immediately, all redundant cloud storage subscriptions must be terminated to align with our new unified infrastructure. Furthermore, the previous incentive structure at Lumina—specifically the quarterly performance bonuses—will be phased out by year-end and replaced with Arisaka’s stock-option vesting program. We recognize that this may cause initial friction, but it is a necessary step for fiscal consolidation. Department heads are expected to submit their revised payroll projections by Friday.

Document 2: Email Correspondence

To: Kenji Sato (ksato@arisakatech.com)
From: Sarah Jenkins (sjenkins@luminasoft.com)
Date: October 14, 2026
Subject: Concerns regarding the memo

Dear Mr. Sato,

Following your memo on Oct 12, I must highlight a potential oversight. Several of our lead developers at Lumina accepted their current roles specifically because of the immediate liquidity offered by the quarterly bonuses. Moving to a vesting program that requires a four-year stay before any payout might trigger an exodus of talent to competitors. Additionally, regarding the cloud storage, we are currently mid-cycle with our contract with ‘SkyBase’, and early termination would incur a 15% penalty fee of the remaining $200,000 contract value. I suggest we delay the consolidation until the SkyBase contract expires in February.

Questions:

  1. What is the primary purpose of Mr. Sato’s memo?
    A) To announce a new software product launch.
    B) To outline cost-cutting measures following a merger.
    C) To introduce Sarah Jenkins as the new head of Lumina.
    D) To explain the benefits of SkyBase cloud services.

  2. In Document 2, the word “exodus” is closest in meaning to:
    A) Mass departure
    B) Rapid promotion
    C) Improvement in skills
    D) Financial gain

  3. What is suggested about the developers at Lumina Software?
    A) They prefer stock options over cash bonuses.
    B) They are unfamiliar with AI-driven logistics.
    C) They might leave the company due to the new payment structure.
    D) They have already terminated their SkyBase subscriptions.

  4. How much would Arisaka Tech Solutions have to pay if they follow the memo’s instruction regarding cloud storage immediately?
    A) $15,000
    B) $30,000
    C) $170,000
    D) $200,000

Passage 2: Scientific Research and Ethical Compliance (Single Passage)

Difficulty: Very High (Focus on dense academic/legal phrasing and tone analysis)

Article: The Journal of Non-Invasive Technology (Excerpt)

The recent pilot study conducted by the University of NAIST concerning emotional perception in remote care has drawn significant scrutiny. While the technological efficacy—achieving a 94% accuracy rate in detecting elderly distress without physical sensors—is commendable, the ethical framework remains contentious. Critics argue that “emotional data” is inherently more sensitive than physiological metrics. The study’s lead researcher, Prof. Hajimu Iida, maintains that the “feeling of being monitored” is drastically reduced when cameras are absent, yet privacy advocates suggest that silent algorithms are, in fact, more insidious. The university has responded by pledging to implement a “transparent data-erasure protocol,” though the specifics of this remain opaque. Funding for the second phase of the project is currently contingent on an independent review by the Ethics Committee of Nara.

Questions:

  1. What does the article imply about the NAIST study?
    A) It was a failure due to low accuracy rates.
    B) It successfully solved all privacy concerns regarding elderly care.
    C) Its technical achievements are overshadowed by ethical debates.
    D) It was primarily funded by a camera manufacturing company.

  2. According to the article, what is the advantage of the new technology?
    A) It is cheaper than traditional physiological sensors.
    B) It removes the need for visible monitoring devices.
    C) It allows for data to be stored indefinitely.
    D) It was developed by the Ethics Committee of Nara.

  3. The author uses the word “opaque” to suggest that the university’s pledge is:
    A) Very clear and detailed.
    B) Historically significant.
    C) Lacking in clarity or detail.
    D) Already being implemented.

Annual Global SE Summit – March 20-22

  • Day 2: Workshop on “React Native for Healthcare” (Suite B)

To: Global SE Summit Organizing Committee
Date: March 5
Item 1: Standard Buffet for 200 guests (Days 1-3) - $12,000
Item 2: Premium Vegan/Nut-free package for 15 guests - $1,500
Total Due: $13,500
Note: We have noted the late request for Item 2. As it was submitted on March 4, a 10% late fee has been applied to the subtotal of Item 2.

To: Event Coordinator

The event was largely a success, particularly the closing gala in the Grand Ballroom. However, I noticed that the premium dietary packages we paid extra for were not clearly labeled during the Day 2 workshop in Suite B, causing confusion for our vegan attendees.
